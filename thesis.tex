\ctparttext{
In this part of the thesis, I introduce the overarching goal, deduce the research questions that guide this work, and give an overview of the remaining document.
Furthermore, I present the relevant literature from social and computer sciences to create an understanding of human behaviour in focused and unfocused interaction with humans, \glspl{artificial agent}, and interactive \glspl{device}.
In doing so, I additionally introduce required terms and concepts.
}
\part{Research Topic}\label{part.research.topic}
\include{chapters/introduction}
\include{chapters/related-work}
\cleardoublepage{}
\ctparttext{
In this part, I investigate human addressing behaviour in interactions with \glspl{smart environment} and \glspl{robot}.
To this end, I evaluate the importance of different features for \gls{addressee} recognition in interactions of \naive{} people with a \gls{robot} inhabited \gls{smart home}.
Furthermore, I present and evaluate a visual approach for \gls{speaker} detection and \gls{addressee} recognition for a \gls{robot} in interaction with a group of people.
}
\part{Addressee in Communicative Acts}\label{part.addressee}
\include{chapters/study-addressee}
\include{chapters/study-meka}
\cleardoublepage{}
\ctparttext{
In this part, I broaden the social skills of \glspl{artificial agent} by investigating how mixed human-agent \glspl{conversational group} can be detected and how the \glspl{conversational role} of the agent within such a group can be recognized.
To this end, I present an appropriate scenario and create a corresponding corpus.
On this basis, I evaluate \gls{ffm} detection as an approach to detecting \glspl{conversational group} in an unconstrained interaction between a group of people and two \glspl{virtual agent} with changing constellations.
Furthermore, I evaluate different approaches to detecting the \gls{conversational role} of the agent in such constellations.
}
